The Student AI Hub

Applied AI took on the creation of a resource where Smeal students can learn about AI in the context of their academics and future careers.

Work in progress

What it is

The Student AI Hub is a website that covers AI basics, how AI shows up in school and professional work, how businesses use it, what tools are available, and the rules, risks, and ethics that come with it. It also includes a section called AI by Smeal Major, which describes how AI is discussed in the context of each of Smeal's 10 undergraduate majors.

The current version is a demo. We are still developing it and working toward something ready for wider use. The content and structure will continue to change as we learn more about what students actually need from a resource like this.

How the foundational content was built

The Hub's core sections -- AI Basics, Using AI for School and Work, How Businesses Use AI, AI Tools, and Rules, Risks, and Ethics -- were generated using retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). That means the AI that drafted the content could only use information from a set of pre-approved sources, not the open internet.

We started with 36 sources that were selected and reviewed by hand. These included academic publications, government frameworks like the NIST AI Risk Management Framework, professional association guidance, and reporting from organizations like the OECD and World Economic Forum. Each source was fetched, stored as a snapshot, and split into smaller chunks. When content was drafted, the system retrieved relevant chunks using keyword-based search (BM25) and passed them to the AI as context. Every paragraph in the output includes a citation back to the chunk it drew from.

The AI could not add sources, browse the web, or generate information beyond what was in the chunks. All drafts were reviewed by people before being published, and the foundational sections are now frozen.

The pipeline

  1. 1Curate -- 36 sources selected and reviewed by hand
  2. 2Fetch and snapshot -- each source downloaded and stored as an immutable record
  3. 3Chunk -- full texts split into readable segments with stable IDs
  4. 4Retrieve -- BM25 keyword search returns the most relevant chunks per topic
  5. 5Draft -- AI generates text using only retrieved chunks, with inline citations
  6. 6Review and lock -- human audit for accuracy, tone, and source balance before publishing
